THE CITY THEATRE: A user-centric website and mobile redesign to help the Austin theatre community discover and indulge their interest for the performing arts.
  • TIMELINE: 4 weeks
  • ROLE: UX Designer
  • TOOLS: Figma, Miro, Invision

PROJECT OVERVIEW

CONTEXT

This was a collaborative project for my UX Bootcamp with The University of Texas at Austin. As UX designers, our task was to analyze The City Theatre website and its users in order to find a solution to visually exemplify their mission. 

THE PROBLEM

The City Theatre's current website lacks charisma and intuitiveness which prevents engagement from potential users. For those users that remain on the website, the navigation isn't fully clickable and creates confusion.
THE SOLUTION
By renovating and refurbishing the City Theatre Company website and mobile versions we can create an intuitive and seamless space for people interested in the arts to gather information about local events, consolidating the community building desire that The City Theatre Company has.
  • How valuable is the current information on The City Theatre's website?
  • What are the expected results or gains from the user interaction with the website?
  • Conducting user research to define our target market, their needs and wants when looking at a local performing arts company.
  • Learn if our user fits in any of these categories: performance arts enthusiast, individuals involved in performance arts, and those who have zero experience with performance arts, but are interested.
  • 60% of users are interested in the performing arts
  • 70% of users have little to no interest towards theatre
  • After compiling our survey and interviews data we found that the majority of our users felt they fit into another category: curios about theatre
USER INSIGHT
Users want a more efficient, effective, and reliable way to obtain information about local theatrical events.

How might we attract users to explore the city theatre's website? By improving the marketing and information accessibility of their website, users will clearly discover the events happening around them. Helping them get immersed in their local theatre scene, resulting in higher user engagement.

USER TESTING

PRIMARY NAVIGATION

After completing a card sorting activity, we decided to perform 3 user tasks to  if our ideas matched the users' needs. The results showed that our proposed site map was effective and easy to navigate. Our users had a general feeling of tasks being simple enough to complete and had a seamless experience. 

LISTENING TO OUR USERS

In addition to simplifying the user navigation, we decided to create a more secure process for users to purchase tickets, view seating options, and explore past, current and upcoming events. These were all concerns that we encountered during our first user interviews.
